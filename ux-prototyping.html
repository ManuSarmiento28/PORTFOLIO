UX / Prototyping

Handshake Profile

Redesigning the student profile experience to better showcase real skills, projects, coursework, and credibility for early-career hiring.

01

What is Handshake?

Handshake is a career platform that connects students with employers. Profiles act as the main evaluation tool for recruiters, especially for internships and entry-level opportunities.

02

Problem Statement

Students are more than a list of courses, but Handshake doesn’t reflect that. Skills appear without proof, course titles lack meaning, and recruiters struggle to understand what students can actually do. This especially affects early-career students who have the most to prove but the least built-in way to show it.

03

User Discovery

6 User Interviews

Students mainly used Handshake reactively only when applying for jobs, not as a long-term profile they continuously build.

Key Insight

Students wanted proof of their work directly inside the application flow, instead of needing external portfolio links.

Competitive Analysis

We analyzed LinkedIn, Portfolium, GitHub, Behance, and Handshake to identify gaps between recruiter trust and visible student work.

04

Our Solution

Skills Learned

Skills become connected to actual coursework and projects, instead of existing as isolated tags.

Tools Used

Students can display software, tools, and platforms used during coursework and projects.

Built-In Projects

The profile becomes a portfolio space where students can upload and showcase real work.

Endorsements

Peer and professor validation creates stronger credibility and social proof for recruiters.

05

Design Process

01

Brainstorming

Initial brainstorming focused on coursework visibility, proof of skills, project uploads, and validation systems.

02

Mind Mapping

We explored relationships between profiles, recruiters, skills, portfolios, and endorsements.

03

Iterations x3

The project evolved through paper prototype, low-fidelity prototype, and final high-fidelity prototype.

04

User Testing

Testing revealed that validation badges and endorsements increased trust and encouraged deeper exploration of profiles.
